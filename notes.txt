If data is unsorted two pointer technique is essentially blind.
If you are using two pointers to find a specific sum or relationship, sorting the data first is almost always a prerequisite.
Sorting gives your pointers "direction." If your current sum is too small, you know exactly which way to move a pointer to make it bigger. If it's too big, you know how to make it smaller. This eliminates the need to scan blindly.
